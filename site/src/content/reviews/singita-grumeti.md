## Why the private concession changes everything

The most important fact about Singita Grumeti is one you will not find in the brochure: the 350,000-acre private wildlife concession means that when you drive toward a lion pride, a river crossing, or a cheetah on a kill — there are no other vehicles.

I have been on safaris in the public Masai Mara where a good sighting attracted 22 Land Cruisers before my guide had finished narrating the behaviour. The animals were there. The experience was compromised.

At Grumeti, I had a June river crossing with two other vehicles — both Singita guests. We watched approximately 800 wildebeest cross the Grumeti River over 40 minutes. The crocodiles were extraordinary. No dust from competing vehicles. No engine noise from late arrivals positioning for a photograph. This is what the price is buying: access, not just accommodation.

## The Grumeti River crossing: what no one tells you

Most travellers comparing Serengeti migration options default to the Mara River crossings in August and September. The logic is straightforward — the Mara crossings have larger herds, more frequency, and better-documented dramatic photography.

Here is what is less discussed: the Grumeti crossings in June–July happen in a narrower river with a permanent population of very large Nile crocodiles. The vegetation on the Grumeti banks is denser, which creates different photographic framing. And you will share this with a fraction of the tourists who fly into Kenya for the Mara crossings.

I would not tell you Grumeti is superior to the Mara — the herd volumes are genuinely smaller. But for the authentic experience of a river crossing without the circus, Grumeti in June is the answer.

## Accommodation: a considered luxury

The Grumeti River Lodge cottages are built from local stone and Zimbabwean teak. The architecture is heavy — more permanent than most East African safari lodges, which is deliberate. In the 1920s aesthetic they have chosen, the interiors feel like a collector's home rather than a hotel room.

My cottage had a private plunge pool overlooking a drainage line where elephant came to drink at dusk. The bathroom was larger than some apartments I have rented in European cities. The king bed faced floor-to-ceiling windows onto the plain.

I will note one honest deduction: the shower pressure was inconsistent in November. One of the maintenance items that drops the score from a theoretical 97 to a 94. These things matter at this price point.

## Food and beverage: genuine farm-to-table, not a claim

Singita operates gardens and suppliers across their properties that genuinely influence what arrives on the plate. The menus change seasonally. The wine programme is Singita's own label from their South African estate plus a curated cellar.

Dinner on my last evening was a Swahili-influenced menu: coconut fish curry cooked with ingredients from the camp kitchen garden, a beef tagine using grass-fed cattle from a local community cooperative, a honey dessert sourced from beehives on the concession boundary. This is not incidental marketing — I asked about the sourcing, and the kitchen team could trace every element.

The bar programme is excellent. My guide and I sat on the deck above the Grumeti River with a single malt at sunset, watching a pod of approximately 40 hippo arrange themselves for the evening. This is the specific type of moment that cannot be manufactured.

## The guides: the decisive variable

The guide assigned to me — I will not name him without his consent — had 14 years of experience in the Serengeti ecosystem. He could identify individual lions by their whisker spot patterns and had tracked three generations of a particular pride.

He did not offer the standard information. He offered what he personally knew from years of continuous observation. The difference between a guide who recites a fact sheet and a guide who knows a specific animal by name is the difference between a good safari and an extraordinary one.

Singita's guide training programme is the reason their properties consistently produce this calibre. It is not accidental.

## Conservation: the Grumeti Fund

The Grumeti Fund's work is independently verifiable — annual reports are public. The canine anti-poaching unit (rangers + tracking dogs) patrols the concession continuously. The ranger numbers and patrol coverage have expanded year-on-year.

The community programme covering 50+ villages in the wildlife corridor is meaningful — not tokenistic. The model incentivises local communities to view the wildlife as an economic asset rather than a competition for land.

I visited the community boma as part of the experience. The income-generation projects (women's cooperative, school programme) are active. I would have been more skeptical if the conservancy let me anywhere near them without prior notice.

## The verdict

Singita Grumeti is the most serious wildlife property I have stayed at in East Africa. The private concession model is the only way to guarantee the silence and density that justify this price. The guiding is the best I have encountered on the continent. The food exceeds what you expect at €3,200/night.

The weaknesses are minor — minor enough to score 94, not 100. At any price point, perfection is a temporary condition. Singita Grumeti is as close as East Africa gets.

**Tim Score: 94 — Exceptional. Book in migration season or calving season specifically. Allow minimum 4 nights.**
